Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Do you collect any personal information?

No. This website does not collect, store, or share any personal data from visitors. We do not use contact forms, sign‑ups, or tracking tools. You can reach us only through the public contact details provided in the website footer.


2. What about the external links on the Resources page?

The links on our Resources page lead to other trusted websites for further learning. We do not control these external sites, their content, or their privacy practices. When you visit them, you follow their own rules and policies, not ours. We share them only as helpful recommendations, and we always aim to link to reliable, authentic sources in sha Allah.


3. What is Islam?

Islam is the religion of submission to the One True God (Allah). It teaches belief in Allah alone and following the guidance revealed through His final Messenger, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

4. What is the Qur'an?

The Qur'an is the final revelation from Allah, revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is the primary source of guidance for Muslims.


5. What are the Five Pillars of Islam?

Shahadah (Declaration of Faith)
Salah (Prayer)
Zakah (Charity)
Sawm (Fasting in Ramadan)
Hajj (Pilgrimage to Makkah)


6. What are the Six Articles of Faith?

Muslims believe in: Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, the Last Day, Divine Decree (Qadr)


7. Why do Muslims pray five times a day?

Prayer is an act of worship that strengthens a Muslim's relationship with Allah and is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.


8. Why do Muslims fast during Ramadan?

Muslims fast to obey Allah, develop self-discipline, increase gratitude, and grow spiritually.


9. What is zakat?

Zakat is an obligatory charity given by eligible Muslims to help those in need and purify wealth.


10. What is Hajj?

Hajj is the pilgrimage to Makkah that every Muslim who is physically and financially able should perform once in their lifetime.


11. What is Tawheed?

Tawheed is the belief that Allah alone deserves all worship and has no partners or equals.


12. Can anyone become a Muslim?

Yes. Anyone who sincerely believes in Allah alone and accepts Muhammad ﷺ as His Messenger can embrace Islam.


13. Do Muslims worship Prophet Muhammad ﷺ?

No. Muslims worship Allah alone. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is Allah's Messenger and is respected and followed, but he is not worshipped.


14. What is a mosque?

A mosque (Masjid) is a place where Muslims gather to worship Allah and pray.


15. Why do Muslim women wear the hijab?

Many Muslim women wear the hijab as an act of obedience to Allah and as an expression of modesty.


16. What does "Allahu Akbar" mean?

It means "Allah is the Greatest."


17. What does "Assalamu Alaikum" mean?

It means "Peace be upon you."


18. Why do Muslims say "In shaa Allah"?

It means "If Allah wills." Muslims say it when speaking about future plans, recognizing that everything happens by Allah's will.


19. Is Islam a religion of peace?

Yes. Islam teaches justice, mercy, compassion, and peace while encouraging believers to uphold righteousness and good character.


20. Where can I learn more about Islam?

Visit our Resources page, where you'll find trusted websites such as Quran.com, Sunnah.com, IslamHouse, and other authentic educational resources.

 

21 Who is Prophet Muhammad ﷺ?

Muhammad ﷺ is the final Prophet and Messenger sent by Allah to guide humanity with the message of Islam.
