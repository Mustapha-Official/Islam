About the Creator

Now let's dive into knowing more about Allah ﷻ

Who is Allah?

Allah is the unique Arabic name for the One and Only True God, Creator, Sustainer, and Ruler of everything that exists. It comes from al‑Ilāh = “the God” — not a special “Muslim god,” but the very same God of all prophets: Adam, Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and Muhammad ﷺ, peace be upon them all.


Core: Tawhid — Absolute Oneness

This is the heart of Islam: Allah is One, with no partner, no child, no parent, no equal, and nothing at all resembles Him. Surah Al‑Ikhlas (112:1‑4) sums it up perfectly:

“Say: He is Allah, the One; Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born, nor is there to Him any equivalent.”



This actually has three parts:

  • Lordship: He alone creates, provides, and governs all things — no one shares His power or authority.
  • Worship: He alone deserves prayer, supplication, love, and obedience — nothing else may be worshipped or feared as He is.
  • Names & Attributes: He alone has perfect, unmatched qualities — we do not compare Him to any creation.


His Perfect Attributes

  • Eternal & Ever‑Living: He had no beginning and will have no end; He is Al‑Hayy, Al‑Qayyum — Self‑Subsisting, all depend on Him while He needs no one.
  • All‑Knowing: He knows every detail — past, present, future, even what is in hearts — Al‑Alim.
  • All‑Powerful: Nothing is impossible for Him; He commands “Be” and it is — Al‑Qadir, Al‑Aziz.
  • Most Merciful & Forgiving: Ar‑Rahman (Merciful to all creation) / Ar‑Rahim (Especially to believers); He loves to pardon sincere repentance — Al‑Ghafur.
  • Self‑Sufficient: Al‑Ghani — He has no needs, while every being relies entirely on Him .
  • Unseen yet Near: He is beyond space and time, not limited by a body or shape, yet closer to you than your own jugular vein; eyes cannot grasp Him, but He sees all .


Most beautiful Names of Allah

He has Al‑Asma‑ul‑Husna — the Most Beautiful Names, about 99 well‑known ones — each revealing a side of His perfection:

  • Al‑Khaliq – The Creator
  • Ar‑Razzaq – The Provider
  • Al‑Malik – The Sovereign Lord
  • Al‑Hakim – The All‑Wise
  • Dhul‑Jalali‑wal‑Ikram – Lord of Majesty and Generosity


Why He matters to us?

He made us, sustains every breath, guides us through messengers and the Quran, and waits for us to turn back to Him. He is the only One who truly listens, helps, and forgives.
